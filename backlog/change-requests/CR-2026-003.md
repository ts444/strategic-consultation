# CR-2026-003: Portfolio gap — no DFIR / 24/7 staffed IR retainer service

**Originating engagement:** Meridian-Logistics
**Observed in phase:** 03-mapping

## Problem

GAP-002 (No Incident Response capability) could only be mapped to Business Continuity as a partial fit (MAP-004). No portfolio service provides a 24/7 staffed IR retainer or full DFIR capability (forensic imaging, malware analysis, IOC identification, ransomware playbooks). This forced a "partially-addresses" rating for the customer's most critical gap, which stems directly from a 2025 paid-ransom ransomware incident. Multiple artifacts explicitly flagged this gap for the retro backlog: MAP-004 notes field, REC-001 rationale (SOC/DFIR gap), and REC-002 rationale (24/7 IR retainer not in portfolio).

The Business Continuity service includes incident containment support, tabletop exercise facilitation, and communication templates, but explicitly excludes a 24/7 staffed IR retainer and full DFIR capability. No other portfolio service fills this gap. Reference: `portfolio://Security/Business_Continuity.md`.

## Proposed change

Add a DFIR / IR Retainer service to the MSP portfolio, or extend the Business Continuity service to include a 24/7-staffed IR retainer tier. If adding a new service, the portfolio file should cover:

- 24/7 staffed incident response retainer with defined SLA
- Full DFIR capability (forensic imaging, malware analysis, IOC identification)
- Integration with SIEM telemetry
- Ransomware-specific response playbooks

## Expected improvement

GAP-002 class gaps can be fully addressed rather than partially addressed. The MSP can offer end-to-end IR coverage without referring customers to third parties for the most critical response phase. The [no-known-service] and "partial fit" rates for IR-related gaps drop to zero.
