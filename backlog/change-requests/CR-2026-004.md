# CR-2026-004: Portfolio gap — no NIS2 entity classification / regulatory advisory service

**Originating engagement:** Meridian-Logistics
**Observed in phase:** 03-mapping

## Problem

GAP-009 (CMP-001 entity classification not validated with national supervisory authority) produced the engagement's single [no-known-service] result (MAP-015). IT Risk Management explicitly excludes legal/regulatory compliance gap assessments beyond IT controls mapping. No portfolio service covers NIS2 self-classification, supervisory authority engagement, or regulatory filing.

The customer must therefore be referred to an external legal/regulatory advisory firm (REC-009), creating a dependency outside the MSP's control for a prerequisite to the customer's NIS2 attestation goal. Reference: `portfolio://Governance/IT_Risk_Management.md`.

## Proposed change

Add a NIS2 Regulatory Advisory service to the MSP portfolio (or a broader Compliance Advisory service covering NIS2 entity classification and supervisory authority engagement). At minimum the service should cover:

- Entity classification self-assessment (essential vs important)
- Supervisory authority notification and registration support
- NIS2 obligation scoping review
- Ongoing regulatory monitoring for classification changes

## Expected improvement

The [no-known-service] rate for NIS2 entity-classification gaps drops to zero. The MSP can offer an end-to-end NIS2 compliance pathway without a third-party referral for the classification prerequisite.
